CPQ Design & Implementation

Client: Fortune 500 Technology Company (PwC Consulting) | Timeline: May 2022 - Oct 2022 | Program Size: $10M+, 70+ consultants

Project Overview

Served dual role as PM + Functional Lead for Salesforce CPQ implementation supporting the client's direct sales and partner resale go-to-market motions in enterprise program with 70+ consultants and 35-person client working team.

Complexity: Indirect/partner channel was most complex workstream because partners required different pricing (channel discounts 20-40%, rebates, co-op funds), different quote templates, and integration with separate fulfillment/billing systems.

My Responsibilities

  • Requirements Translation: Converted 200+ business requirements from Sales Ops, Pricing, Finance, Product into technical user stories for development team
  • Pricing Logic Design: Multi-layered pricing (list → regional adjustments → partner tier discounts → volume discounts → deal registration → special pricing with approval workflows)
  • Approval Workflows: Designed matrix balancing speed with governance (<15% auto-approved, 15-25% Manager approval, 25-35% Director, 35%+ VP, custom bundles require Product approval)
  • System Integration: Defined integration requirements for CPQ ↔ CRM, CPQ → ERP (order fulfillment), CPQ → Billing (partner invoicing, rebates)
  • Change Management: Multi-tiered training (internal users → partner-facing teams → partner end users), phased rollout (pilot → top 50 → long tail)

Delivery Approach

Led Agile methodology with phased rollouts, managed 5-person workstream, coordinated with other workstreams (direct sales, catalog, integrations), presented to client executives.

Impact

Enabled the client to scale partner channel without proportional headcount growth, standardized pricing across regions, improved quote accuracy and cycle time (typical CPQ implementations achieve 50-70% turnaround reduction, 80-90% error reduction).

Sabre: API Re-Monetization

Company: Sabre Corporation (B2B SaaS Airline Solutions) | Timeline: 2019-2021

The Pricing Misalignment

Sabre's Passenger Service System (PSS) was priced per-passenger-boarded, but profitability was actually driven by API calls (millions monthly). Airlines with high API usage relative to passengers were unprofitable customers, while the pricing model couldn't capture the value.

Strategic Insight: Product team analysis showed inconsistent profitability. Finance revealed costs tied to API calls (reservation creation, cancellations, modifications) not passengers. Value metric misaligned with cost structure.

My Approach

  • Data Analysis: Categorized millions of monthly API calls by value (creation vs. cancellation vs. check-in), worked with Finance for revenue recognition compliance
  • Two-Track Pricing: (A) Preferred: per-API-call with tiered rates, (B) Legacy bridge: per-passenger with overage fees for excessive API usage
  • Migration Strategy: Grandfather existing customers on Track B, incent new customers to Track A, provide migration path

Impact

  • 60% of customers migrated to Track A (per-API-call) within 18 months
  • 40% stayed on Track B but overage fees protected margin on high-API-usage customers
  • Accidentally created professional services revenue stream (airlines needed help optimizing API usage)

Additional Achievements at Sabre

10% Margin Uplift Discovery: Identified underpriced product through analytics - product with <5% Custom Deal Desk involvement meant Sales wasn't negotiating (product too easy to sell). Recommended 10% price increase despite Sales resistance ("You're taking away our one easy sell!"). Secured VP approval, result: immediate 10% margin uplift on 95% of deals with minimal win rate impact (85% → 82%).

Pricing Council Leadership: Chaired monthly Pricing Council for PSS products, collaborating with Product, Finance, Sales, Engineering on strategic pricing decisions.
