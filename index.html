How I Think About Pricing

Pricing Is a Balancing Act.
Most Organizations Are Off-Balance.

Most pricing failures aren't data problems. They're balance problems. An organization over-indexes on one dimension — margin, market share, or positioning — and wonders why the other two aren't working. Sustainable pricing sits at the intersection of three forces that pull in different directions. The job is to hold that center while the business changes around it.

The Problem

In practice, most organizations have a pricing motion. What they rarely have is a pricing system built to evolve. Best practices in pricing architecture are not static -- market dynamics shift, product portfolios grow, and Sales motions change. The gap between organizations that price reactively and those that price strategically is almost always an architectural one: governance structures that create accountability, data infrastructure that surfaces the right signals, and a value metric that actually reflects how customers buy.

The Pricing Misalignment

Strategic Insight: Costs tied to API calls (reservation creation, cancellations, modifications), not passengers. Value metric misaligned with cost structure.

Approach

  • Categorized millions of monthly API calls by value type
  • Designed two-track model: preferred per-API-call and legacy bridge with overage fees
  • Migration strategy: grandfather Track B, incent new customers to Track A

Impact

  • 60% migrated to Track A within 18 months
  • Overage fees protected margin on remaining Track B customers
  • Created unplanned professional services revenue stream
